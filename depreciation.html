Vehicle Market
Decide when to buy, when to sell, through the data.

There is always a rule when it comes to buying a next car. Do not ever buy a new car. Always buy a pre-owned car. The reason why people want to avoid purchasing new cars is to avoid the depreciation of the vehicle. However, it is hard to tell through words. What's wrong with a new car? Let's see one of the example to demonstrate how depreciation works on certain cars. As an example, we will be using M5 model from BMW.

In AIFA, we collect vehicle market data through web-scraping and other existing reliable sources to extract vehicle information such as price, current miles, year, model, brand, VIN, vehicle history data, maintenance list, and more.

The above data table demonstrates how fast the BMW M5 model depreciate just for driving 10,000 miles. Within 10,000 miles, BMW M5 lost its value at least 20,000 dollars! If you ask around why people don't really buy brand new BMW M5 from a dealership, that is the reason. People, as a consumer, don't want to deal with the huge deprecation.
But not all cars always have depreciation rate like this. Instead of looking at BMW M5 model, we are going to look into BMW M3 model.

Initially, you almost lost 10,000 dollars for first 10,000 miles. That's almost 1 mile per 1 dollar. Not only that, from 11,664 miles to 16844 miles, we see minimal amount of loss in its value. Now we learned how depreciation works and see through data, we want to visualize in different way to solve our problem.
Buy the car when it's young to avoid break downs and avoid huge depreciation at the same time.
